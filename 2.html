Visual Analysis of Wikipedia Edits

As a project for the Visual Analytics class, I built different interactive visualizations to solve the VAST 2008 mini challenge 1. The problem focused on identifying the different factions of a political movement based on wikipedia edit logs of users. I built different visualizations that help identify different key words of interest and build a conflict network based on them. The conflict network is an interactive visualization which identifies the players in all the factions including wikipedia bots and neutral users. This conflict network can be used to analyze any type of conflict and on different types of text data like tweets, code repository commits, etc.

Medical Adherence Device

TEL-ME-BOX

During the final year of undergraduate degree, I worked on a NIH (USA) funded project with University of California, San Francisco and St. Johns Research Institute. We developed TEL-ME-BOX, a medical adherence device developed to be used to collect adherence data for the research study. The device is designed to bring down the cost of conducting such a study. With a manufacturing cost of $35 it is much cheaper than other options. The device has a 3D printed case and is small enough to fit in pocket. The device is powered by a custom designed board that has an onboard GSM module and is powered by an ATMEGA 328 microprocessor. The device communicates via SMS and the data is easily accessible on an online portal.
